The Problem

Asana was built for tech teams. Trello was built for simple workflows. Monday was built for anyone with a budget.

None of them were built for the way a creative agency actually runs.

You do not manage tasks. You manage briefs, revisions, approvals, client moods, and three deadlines that were all supposed to be the top priority.

Flowdesk was built for that reality. Not the sanitized version of it.

Feature Callouts

Brief-to-delivery in one place. From the first client brief to the final file handoff, every conversation, asset, revision, and approval lives in one thread. Nothing gets lost in email. Nothing gets rebuilt from memory.

Revision rounds that do not spiral. Set revision limits per project, track every round, and give clients a clear view of where their project stands without a single status call.

Timelines clients can actually see. Share a live project view with clients that updates in real time. No more "can you send me an update" messages at 4 PM on a Friday.

About Page

Flowdesk was not built in a boardroom.

It was built in a studio. By people who spent years watching talented creative teams waste hours every week inside tools that were never designed for how they work.

The brief arrives. The scope shifts. The client has notes. The designer has questions. The deadline moves. And somewhere in the middle of all of that, someone has to hold the whole thing together.

That person deserves a tool that was actually designed for that job.

We built Flowdesk because the best creative work in the world is not happening inside Jira. It is happening in agencies where the people are brilliant but the systems are borrowed from industries that look nothing like theirs.

We are fixing that. One agency at a time.

Services Page

Project and brief management. Every project starts with a brief. Flowdesk keeps it connected to every task, revision, and delivery that follows so nothing gets disconnected as the project moves.

Client portals. Give clients a single place to see progress, leave feedback, and approve deliverables. No email chains. No "where are we on this" calls.

Revision tracking. Know exactly how many revision rounds each project has used, which ones are still open, and what was changed and when.

Time and resource tracking. See where your team's hours are going before you run out of them. Spot the projects that consistently overrun and understand why.

Integrations. Flowdesk connects with the tools your team already uses including Figma, Google Drive, Slack, and Adobe Creative Cloud so nothing has to be manually duplicated.
