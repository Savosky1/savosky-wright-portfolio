Onboarding Sequence — Email 1 of 3 Written for First-Time Crypto Users

Subject: You just created your first crypto wallet. Here is what nobody tells you next.

Preview text: The part after signup is where most people get lost. Let us fix that.

Hi there,

You did the hard part.

You signed up. You verified your identity. You stared at a string of letters and numbers that is apparently your wallet address and wondered if you were supposed to memorise it.

You are not. But we will get to that.

First, welcome. Seriously. Most people think about getting into crypto for months before they actually do it. The fact that you are here means you made a decision most people only talk about.

Now here is the part the internet forgets to explain.

Your wallet is not a bank account.

This sounds obvious until it is not.

A bank account holds your money and the bank is responsible for keeping it safe. If something goes wrong, there is a process. There is a customer service line. There is a dispute system.

A crypto wallet works differently. Your wallet holds the keys that prove you own your crypto. Nobody else holds those keys. Not us. Not any exchange. Not any government. Just you.

That is the point of it. But it also means something important.

If you lose access to your wallet and you have not backed up your recovery phrase, nobody can get it back for you. Not us. Not anyone.

This is not meant to scare you. It is meant to make sure you do the one thing that matters most before you do anything else.

The one thing to do before anything else

Find your recovery phrase.

It is a set of 12 or 24 words that were generated when you created your wallet. They appear in a specific order and that order is everything.

Write them down on paper. Not on your phone. Not in a notes app. Not in an email to yourself. Paper. Store that paper somewhere safe. Some people use two copies in two different locations.

This phrase is the only way to recover your wallet if you lose your phone, forget your password, or need to access your funds from a new device.

Everything else in crypto can wait. This cannot.

What is coming next

Over the next two emails we will cover two things that will make your first weeks in crypto significantly less confusing.

Email 2 — How to receive crypto safely and what to check before you send anything to anyone.

Email 3 — How to read your wallet activity so you always know exactly what happened to your funds and when.

None of it is complicated. It just needs to be explained by someone who remembers what it felt like to be new to this.

See you in a few days.

The VaultMail Team

P.S. If you have a question right now, reply to this email. We read every one.
