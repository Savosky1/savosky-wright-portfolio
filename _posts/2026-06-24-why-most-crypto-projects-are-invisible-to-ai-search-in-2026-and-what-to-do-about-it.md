Your Twitter spaces are packed. Your Discord has hundreds of people showing up every day. You even have content ranking on Google.

By every standard you know, your project looks visible online.

But here's what most Web3 founders don't realize until it's already too late.

A new investor opens ChatGPT today and types something like "what are the best Layer 2 protocols right now" or "compare DeFi projects in this niche". These are real searches happening every single day in 2026.

And when that answer comes back, your project is not in it.

Not because you built something bad. Not because your community is small or your team is not working hard enough.

But because the content you worked hardest to create, the Discord conversations, the Telegram updates, the Twitter threads, all of it lives in places AI tools cannot even access.

And if AI cannot access it, then to AI it simply does not exist.

A 2025 report found that 91 percent of blockchain brands are completely invisible in AI search. Meaning if you've never thought about this before, there's a very high chance your project is one of them.

# Why Your Google Ranking Is Not Helping You Here

Most founders think that if their project is already ranking on Google, then AI tools will automatically pick it up too. It sounds logical. Google is the biggest search engine. AI tools use the internet. So it feels like everything should overlap.

But that's not how it works at all.

When someone asks ChatGPT or Perplexity a question, they are not getting a list of links to go and click. They are getting one answer. A single response built from everything these tools have seen and can access on the open web.

So they are not ranking your page.

They are deciding if your project is even worth mentioning in the answer.

And that decision has nothing to do with your keyword rankings on Google.

There was a research that showed ChatGPT failed to mention any TON ecosystem DeFi protocols in 87 percent of DeFi related queries. These were real projects. Real TVL. Real communities. Some of them were even ranking on Google.

But they were still missing in AI answers.

Not because they were fake or weak projects. But because AI tools could not find enough credible open web content to confidently recommend them.

And that's the real difference here.

Google is a ranking problem.

AI search is a recommendation problem.

And most crypto projects are still only building for the first one.

# Here Is Why Discord and Telegram Are Actually Hurting Your Visibility

![](/images/uploads/images-1-.jpeg)

This is the part most founders have not really connected yet.

The Web3 playbook was built around closed platforms. You build a Discord. You grow a Telegram group. You push updates, alpha, and long discussions inside those communities. Your most valuable content ends up going to your most engaged users.

But AI crawlers cannot get inside Discord. They cannot read your Telegram groups. Even Twitter threads are not fully reliable in terms of crawlability depending on how AI tools are accessing data at that time.

So all of that community energy, all those detailed founder updates, all those explanations of tokenomics and roadmap that your community actually engages with, none of it is feeding the AI systems that are now deciding how projects get discovered.

And that's where it starts to get worse.

Even when crypto projects do publish on the open web, most of what they publish is just promotion. Announcements. Partnership posts. Countdown tweets. The usual stuff.

But research is already showing something important here.

Purely promotional content does not perform well in AI citation.

AI tools are not looking for hype. They are looking for explanation. Content that actually answers questions. Content that helps someone understand what the project is and why it matters.

And this is where most projects miss it.

A project that clearly explains itself on the open web will always outperform a project that only hypes itself inside closed platforms when it comes to AI visibility.

# How AI Actually Decides Which Crypto Projects to Recommend

![](/images/uploads/2bce07a8b5c07950ba29df455a96c449.jpg)

Once you understand this, everything starts to make sense differently.

AI tools like ChatGPT, Perplexity, Claude, and Gemini are not randomly picking projects. They are building recommendations based on a few clear signals.

The first one is how often your project shows up across credible open web sources. Blog posts. Independent reviews. Crypto media articles. Public Reddit discussions. The more your project appears in places AI can actually read and trust, the more these systems start to treat it like something real and established.

The second one is consistency.

If your project is described in different ways across different places, AI tools struggle to form a clear understanding. A whitepaper saying one thing, a blog post saying another, a founder interview explaining it differently, it all creates confusion.

And when there is confusion, AI does not recommend you.

The third one is structure.

AI tools prefer content that directly answers questions. A page that clearly explains “what this project is and how it works” will always get picked more than a press release talking about a partnership or announcement.

And this is where most projects miss it completely.

A report from 5W PR in May 2026 found that Coinbase and Kraken alone captured 22 percent of all crypto AI citations across ChatGPT, Claude, Perplexity, Gemini, and Google AI Overviews.

These are not random numbers.

These are brands with years of open web content, consistent messaging, and strong third party coverage.

That is not luck.

That is strategy.

# What the Crypto Projects Getting Cited Are Doing Differently

The projects showing up in AI answers are not always the biggest or the most funded.

They are the ones that understood early that AI visibility is not the same thing as the old Web3 playbook.

It works differently.

They publish real explanatory content on the open web. Not just announcements. Not just hype posts. But proper articles that explain what the protocol actually does, how it compares to other options, what problems it solves, and who it is actually for.

Content that lives on their own site. On crypto publications. On Medium. On places AI crawlers can actually access and read.

Then they build third party coverage.

Because when independent writers and crypto journalists talk about your project on platforms AI already trusts, that becomes signal.

And one credible third party article often does more for your AI visibility than ten posts from your own team.

They also stay consistent.

Every piece of content describes the project in the same way. Same message. Same positioning. Same explanation of what they are building.

That consistency is what helps AI tools form a clear understanding of what the project actually is.

And finally, they structure content properly.

They answer questions directly. No long setup. No unnecessary intro. They just open with clear explanations of what the project is and how it works.

Because that is what AI tools can actually pull and cite instantly.

# What to Do Starting This Week

You do not need to rebuild everything overnight.

But you do need to start shifting where your most important content actually lives.

Take your best Discord updates. Your most detailed founder explanations. Your roadmap breakdowns. And turn them into proper blog articles or web pages that anyone on the internet can read.

Clean. Public. Crawlable content.

This is what AI tools can actually find and cite.

Before you publish anything, ask yourself one question.

Is this explaining something clearly or is it just announcing something?

Because AI tools reward the first one. And they mostly ignore the second.

Then reach out to crypto publications. Get your project covered by independent writers. Show up in public Reddit discussions.

Every credible third party mention of your project on the open web becomes a signal that your project is real and worth recommending.

Then check your visibility properly.

Open ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini. Type the exact questions your target investors would ask when looking for a project like yours.

See what comes back.

If your project is not showing up in those answers, then you already know why.

The next wave of capital entering crypto in 2026 is not starting from Google first anymore.

It is starting from AI tools.

People are asking ChatGPT which projects are trustworthy. They are using Perplexity to compare protocols. They are letting AI do the research that used to happen on Google.

And if your project is not in those answers, that wave is already passing you by.

Most of your competitors have not figured this out yet.

Which means right now, before this becomes normal, is actually the best time to start.
