FortressNames — Explained for Real People

Brief: A product explainer written for American Fortress explaining the FortressNames feature to everyday crypto users. Written for someone who has experienced address anxiety or sent to a wrong address before. No technical jargon. No assumed knowledge. Just plain language that makes the feature and its importance immediately clear.

4 min read Written for Everyday Crypto Users

Sending crypto to a wrong address is permanent. FortressNames makes sure it never happens to you.

The Problem

Every time you send crypto you copy a string of characters that looks like this.

0x4f8EF7a93B2c1D6e5F0a9B3c8D2e7F1a4B6c9E2

You paste it. You check it. Maybe you check it twice. You hold your breath and hit send.

That feeling has a name in the crypto community. Address anxiety. And it exists because one wrong character, one misplaced letter, one moment of inattention sends your money somewhere it can never come back from.

According to Chainalysis, over 3.7 million Bitcoin alone, worth tens of billions of dollars, has been permanently lost due to sending errors and inaccessible wallets. A significant portion of those losses came from address mistakes that took less than a second to make.

The crypto industry spent fifteen years building faster transactions, cheaper fees, and stronger security. Nobody fixed the address.

American Fortress fixed the address.

What FortressNames Does

FortressNames replaces your wallet address with a name you choose.

Instead of sending to 0x4f8EF7a93B2c1D6e5F0a9B3c8D2e7F1a4B6c9E2, you send to alex.fortress or sarah.fortress or whatever name you register.

The name is human readable. It is easy to verify. It is impossible to mistype in a way that accidentally matches someone else's address. And it works across every chain that American Fortress supports without you needing to think about which network you are on.

What Happens Behind It

FortressNames is not a cosmetic change. Behind every name is a stealth address system powered by zero knowledge proofs.

When someone sends to your FortressName, the transaction resolves to a one-time stealth address. The blockchain records the transaction but nobody watching the chain can connect it to your name, your identity, or your transaction history.

Your FortressName is public. Your financial activity is not.

This matters because most wallet addresses are permanent and traceable. Send from the same address repeatedly and anyone with basic chain analysis tools can build a detailed picture of your financial life. Who paid you. What you spent. How much you hold.

FortressNames breaks that trail by design. Not as an optional setting. By default.

Who This Is Built For

FortressNames is for the person who has ever double-checked a wallet address three times and still felt nervous pressing send.

It is for the freelancer getting paid in crypto who does not want their entire income history visible to every client they have ever worked with.

It is for the family sending remittances across borders who should not have to choose between financial privacy and financial accessibility.

It is for anyone who believes that using crypto should not mean giving the entire internet a window into your financial life.

How to Get Your FortressName

Open American Fortress. Go to your profile. Select Register FortressName. Choose a name that is available. Confirm the registration.

From that moment, every transaction sent to your FortressName arrives privately, accurately, and without the address anxiety that has followed crypto users since 2009.

One name. Every chain. Complete privacy. By default.
