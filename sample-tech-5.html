Flowdesk Revision Tracking — Explained for Real People

Brief: A product explainer written for creative agency project managers explaining Flowdesk's revision tracking feature in plain language. No jargon, no technical documentation. Written for the person managing six active clients who needs to understand exactly what the feature does and why it matters.

4 min read Written for Creative Agency Project Managers

Every creative project has a revision limit. Most agencies just never know when they have hit it.

The Problem

Here is how revision rounds usually go.

The client sees the first draft. They have feedback. You make the changes. They see the second version. They have more feedback. Some of it is new. Some of it contradicts the last round. You make the changes again. They come back with notes from their boss who was not in the original brief meeting.

At some point someone on your team asks quietly how many rounds you agreed to. Nobody is quite sure. The contract says three. You are pretty sure you are on round five. But nobody tracked it and now the conversation is awkward.

This happens in almost every creative agency. Not because the teams are disorganised. Because revision tracking is genuinely hard to do inside tools that were not built for it.

What Flowdesk Revision Tracking Does

When a project is set up in Flowdesk, you define the number of revision rounds included in the scope. That number stays visible throughout the project.

Every time a client submits feedback, Flowdesk logs it as a new revision round. It timestamps the submission, captures the feedback in full, and links it directly to the specific deliverable it applies to. The round counter updates automatically.

When the agreed number of rounds is reached, Flowdesk flags it. Not in a confrontational way. Just clearly. Your project manager sees it. The client portal reflects it. Everyone is looking at the same number at the same time.

No spreadsheet. No scrolling through email threads. No guessing.

What This Means in Practice

Your team knows exactly where they stand on every project at any point in the day. They are not piecing together revision history from Slack messages and email attachments. It is all in one place, attached to the project, visible to anyone who needs to see it.

When a client pushes for one more round of changes beyond what was agreed, your project manager has a clear, documented timeline to reference. The conversation stops being about memory and starts being about facts. That is a different conversation entirely.

And when it comes to billing for out-of-scope revisions, you have the receipts. Timestamps. Round numbers. What was submitted and when.

Who This Is Built For

Revision tracking in Flowdesk was built for the project manager managing six active clients. For the account manager who has to go back to a client and explain why round four was not in the original quote. For the studio director trying to understand why the profitable project on paper always feels expensive to deliver.

It was not built for enterprise workflows or development sprints. It was built for the specific, recurring, expensive problem that creative agencies deal with on every single project.

The Difference It Makes

According to a 2024 report by Deltek, scope creep is the number one profitability killer for creative agencies, with untracked revision rounds accounting for an average of 23 percent of unbilled hours per project.

Flowdesk Revision Tracking does not eliminate scope creep. Clients will always want one more round. But it gives your team the clarity and the documentation to handle that conversation professionally rather than awkwardly.

That is not a small thing. In an agency running 20 projects a month, it is the difference between a team that feels in control and a team that is always slightly behind.

How to Turn It On

Open any project in Flowdesk. Go to project settings. Set your revision limit. Every round from that point is tracked automatically.

That is it. No configuration. No integrations to set up. No training required.

If you are already mid-project and want to start tracking from here, you can log previous rounds manually. Flowdesk will treat them as the starting point and count forward from there.
